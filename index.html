About

Hey there! I'm Jasmine, a UX/UI designer and front-end developer.

As a kid, I loved creating art and playing video games, especially exploring the complex worlds within both. I decided to study graphic design in university because I thought that I would learn how to design games. From there, I discovered - and was captivated by - the practice of UX/UI design. It allowed me to put my artistic skills to good use, and I could design fun experiences within complex systems! After learning how to design better experiences, I started studying computer science and programming so I could learn how to develop them too.

Although my main focus is UX/UI design, I try to be involved as much as possible throughout the entire product development process. I have helped manage projects, do quality assurance testing, and even develop features. All of this has made me a better designer and makes me appreciate all of the work that goes into a product.

So - I still enjoy art and games. However, most of my current studies revolve around UX/UI design, game design, and programming. My non-design-related hobbies include learning Cantonese and Mandarin, reading, and cooking, but they still find their way into my design work somehow. :)

Now, how do you design less frustrating experiences with printers...?
