LiPS Logos

A set of logos establishing a new identity for a new faculty cluster


Overview

These logos for the Leadership in Public Science - an emerging faculty cluster at North Carolina State University were designed to convey their theme of connections and spreading knowledge while still adhering to the NCSU brand guidelines and using imagery associated with science.


Process

Mission

Design a visual identity for the Leadership in Public Science that doesn't conflict with the main NCSU brand and doesn't use cliche scientific imagery.


Playthrough

After gathering key themes from the cluster's website, reading up on the branding guidelines, and finding imagery associated with public science and NCSU, I began ideating. My first idea featured many linked nodes spread across the earth in a petri dish. However, I learned that the Leadership in Public Science's mission isn't (currently) global, so I had to revise that. At the suggestion of the cluster, I studied different types of slime mold and based the logo on its creeping nature. I also experimented with different imagery, such a circle of wolves resembling petri dish blobs and hexagons (commonly used to represent molecular bonds), but I found the spreading circle nodes worked best.

Once I determined the general visual look, I had another challenge - how do I give it dimension (make it look like a petri dish) when I'm limited to only two colors? I then had to play around with different line weights, consistencies in line weights (uniform vs. tapered), and amounts of overlap. We eventually settled on a design where the top of the petri dish was a circle with uniform stroke on top of another circle with a lighter (but still uniform) stroke.


Results

The final logo depicts cells in a petri dish connecting to each other and disseminating knowledge. The largest cell is the Leadership in Public Science, since they are supposed to be the hub or catalyst. The logo comes in different versions - CMYK and RGB; PNG, RGB, and EPS; red and black; standalone, vertical, and horizontal. That allows the logo to be used in different situations.

Logo use (per the NCSU branding guidelines) :
PNG (RGB) and EPS (RGB) are used for the web.
EPS (CMYK) is used for print.
JPG (RGB) is only used in PowerPoint presentations or Microsoft Office documents.
