Folds of History

An augmented reality dashboard app educating students on the history of origami


(Click here to open screens at full size)

Overview

Folds of History is an augmented reality tablet-based dashboard that high-school students can use to learn about this history of origami - beginning when paper was invented to today - at their own pace. It's designed to be used at the North Carolina Museum of Art as part of an ongoing exhibition. As they explore the walking trails outside of the museum, glowing origami objects will appear on their screens. If they touch one of the objects with their hand, it will unfold to reveal the history relating to that object. From there, they can tap on individual historical events to reveal in-depth descriptions.


Process

Mission

Develop a prototype storyboard for a tablet-based dashboard that allows middle- or high-school students to investigate (with agency) cause and effect relationships revolving around a historical event or period.


Playthrough

When studying origami, I didn't know it had so many uses and such a long, complex history. Through extensive research, I learned a lot about how it evolved separately in the East and West, the impact of Sadako Sasaki, and applications of origami besides ornamentation. Thus, I decided the app should also impart this knowledge to change people's perspectives of origami. Taking a page from Pok√©mon Go, I designed the app to use augmented reality - the app would be more interesting if people could go outside and explore like a scavenger hunt. I then did more research to find origami figures to represent each time period or event - each "exhibit" - and ideated possible gestures and animations to make things more exciting.


Results

(Note: Images may not render correctly in browser. Sometimes there are strange transparent boxes where there shouldn't be any. Images are accurate in Adobe Acrobat however.)


These screens tell the story of a student trying to learn about the history of the crane. First, they open their map on the app and tap on the different glowing markers to learn which time period each of them represent. They find the one they're looking for - Sadako Sasaki and Peach Movements - and they exit their map and menu (denoted by a lucky star in the corner). As they walk along the path, they see many origami objects floating about with inviting glows.

At one point, they become disoriented and stand still in the same place for a little while. The dashboard, interpreting the student's inactivity as them being lost, makes the edge of the screen glow to hint at the direction to look in to find the closest origami figure. They face that direction and see the next figure. As they continue to turn the screen, they finally locate the crane.

Once the student is close enough, the title of the exhibit pops out of the crane. The student pokes the paper crane and it unfolds to reveal a more detailed history. Events are connected by a timeline following the creases in the paper. There's also a little square in the corner that they can tap to learn how to fold the crane. Because the student wants to view the history on the other side of the paper, they put their hand over it and flip their hand - the gesture causes the paper to also flip over. They decide to investigate The Unity Club and tap on the item to reveal more information about it. After they tap on the screen to close the window, they refold the crane by having their hand hover over the paper and closing their hand into a fist.

The last image just shows how the app would look in context (with an iPad frame).
