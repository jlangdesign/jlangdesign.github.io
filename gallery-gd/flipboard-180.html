Flipboard 180°

A new look and motion branding for Flipboard



(Hover over each image to view its respective animation.)


Overview

The current Flipboard logo and its animation conveys its brand pretty well, but what if Flipboard wanted to change it up a bit? Flipboard 180° is a collection of new animations for a hypothetical new Flipboard brand identity.


Process

Mission

Design "expected," "unexpected," and "sub-brand" animations for Flipboard's logo using motion as branding. You may use the original logo or create a new one.


Playthrough

Since I wasn't very familiar with Flipboard, I decided to research it before I started brainstorming ideas for animations. For those of you that don't know, Flipboard is an app that collects curated content from sources worldwide based on your personal interests and aggregates it into a digital magazine. These magazines are easy to collect, read, and share. What distinguishes it from other news aggregators is that readers navigate these pages via the "flip" - the core gesture. This is also reinforced in its logo animation and, of course, the app's name. Therefore, I knew "flipping" would be a key concept in my motion branding ideas. After watching a video on Flipboard's brand development, I discovered other concepts important to the brand - "clean," "structured," and "magical." Now that I had enough concepts to work with, I began hand-sketching animations that incorporated all of these ideas.

Most of my initial ideas used a letter "F" made of nine squares (three wide, five high); this was derived from the 5x5 grid used to create the original logo. To convey the "magical" aspect, they created optical illusions and required changing ("flipping") perspectives. However, I found these too complex for short branding animations. I then explored simpler motions related to page-flipping by making paper prototypes. While exploring different types of flipping, I tried to keep the motions clean yet surprising.


Results

The new logo is a less abstract - yet still modular - letter F with “expected,” “unexpected,” and “sub-brand” animations. In the “expected” motion, a page of a book opens out of nowhere, unfolding an “F” as it flips open. For the “unexpected” motion, a page appears out of nowhere again, but this time, it flips over in a curve to reveal the “F.” Finally, the “sub-brand” animation depicts a fictional sub-brand for Kickstarter projects called “Pushboard.” A block suddenly appears and pushes a block in the “F” to create a “P.” All three of these contain characteristics of the current brand. They each feature the “flip” - and feel clean, modern, and magical (because elements manifest out of nothing). However, the “expected” animation conveys a more structured feel and is reminiscent of Flipboard’s current animation, whereas the “unexpected” animation is more fluid.
