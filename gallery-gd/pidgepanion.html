Pidgepanion

A system that helps travelers make and maintain international friendships


Overview

Two of the most rewarding parts of travel are leaving oneâ€™s comfort zone and interacting with locals. Unfortunately for many, that experience ends once they return home. Social media interactions with international friends are superficial at best. Text and phone conversations gradually become less frequent because of lack of engagement and time until they stop altogether. The friendships fade away as both sides become irrelevant to each other. These problems are further compounded by introversion. Having to constantly initiate interactions and keep conversations going can be exhausting, causing one to wonder if these friendships are worth the trouble. With this project, I propose a tangible user interface (TUI) system that helps introverted college-aged solo travelers not only befriend locals in the countries they visit but also maintain these friendships despite cultural differences and geographical distance.

Pidgepanion is a system of interactive media that helps introverted college-aged solo travelers not only befriend locals but maintain those friendships once they return home. It is composed of a mobile app that initializes contact prior to travel, a keychain that facilitates communication while abroad, and a lamp interface that encourages keeping in touch after returning home. By closing the physical and social distance between locals and travelers, Pidgepanion hopes to promote cultural understanding and create a world without borders.
